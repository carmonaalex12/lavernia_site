NSF DMREF: Engineering Strength and Thougness into Metals

Co Pls: Julie M. Schoenung, Subhash Mahajan, UC Davis; Mo Li, Georgia Tech

Collaborators: Dr. Irene Beyerlein, Los Alamos National Laboratory; Prof. Tim Rupert, UC Irvine

  • Solving the long-standing problem of strengthening and toughening in HCP polycrystalline metals via a novel concept of “twin mesh engineering.”
  • A synergistic experimental-modelling approach aims to understand, (1) Nucleation and growth of twins, (2) Interactions between slip and twinning, and (3) Twin-twin interactions

NSF GOALI: Influence of Length-Scale on Diffusion during FAST (Field-Assisted Sintering Technology)

Co Pls: Dr. Anil Sachdev, Global R&D, General Motors

Collaborators: Prof. Kaustubh Kulkarni, Indian Institute of Technology

  • Supports fundamental research on the influence of length-scale on diffusion phenomena during SPS sintering of Ti and Ti alloys powders for novel materials

ARO:Nanostructured High Entropy Alloys

Collaborators: Prof. Horst Hahn, Karlsruhe Institute of Technology,

Prof. Weiping Chen, South China University of Technology;

Profs. Fei Chen and Lianmeng Zhang, Wuhan University of Technology

  • Fundamental investigation into the technological issues and scientific phenomena in nanostructured HEAs
  • Explore design principles that can ultimately be used to produce engineered phases and microstructures to attain strength and ductility values that exceed conventional alloys

Sandia National Laboratories: Novel Synthesis and Processing of Bulk Nanocrystalline Iron Nitride(Fe N)

  • Investigating the synthesis of bulk nanocrystalline FeN magnetic material using mechanical alloying followed by field-assisted sintering technology
  • Establish an understanding of FeN processing and microstructure relationship
